The lab investigation focused on two essential research questions which examined the relationship between model complexity and machine learning performance while learning methods to detect both underfitting and 
overfitting. The research utilized polynomial regression models at various degree levels to evaluate their performance through graphical representations and learning curve analysis. The degree 1 model functioned as 
an extremely basic system while the degree 4 model offered an appropriate level of system complexity and the degree 15 model operated at an advanced level of system complexity. The training and cross-validation 
scores revealed that simpler models struggled to detect patterns while complex models stored all training data irregularities. The study results indicated that the degree 4 model provided the best generalization 
performance because machine learning model design requires engineers to find the correct balance between bias and variance.
