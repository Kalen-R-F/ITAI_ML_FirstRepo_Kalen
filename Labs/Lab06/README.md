The main objective of my lab work was to predict the continuous fare amount that Titanic passengers paid through analysis of the dataset's other variables. I started my research work after I finished my work of 
cleaning the dataset and I began my work of handling missing data through median imputation while I selected important numerical variables for analysis. The relationship between these features and the fare was 
learned through the development of a Linear Regression model which I trained. I evaluated the model performance through mean squared error which I used to determine the distance between predicted results and actual 
outcomes. The results showed that the model could capture general trends in the data, but with some error, meaning it worked reasonably well but was not perfectly accurate. The research showed that regression models 
can predict actual numerical results from real-world situations, despite data errors and noise disturbances.
